Session Report — Generation Layer Evaluation and the Grounding-Prompt Experiments

Date: 19 July 2026 · Corpus: Kibuga (5 PDFs, 47 chunks) · Model: Qwen2.5-3B-Instruct, local Ollama, CPU-only, fully offline

Retriever: BM25 + bge-small-en-v1.5, RRF k=60, k=3 chunks into context (locked, DECISION-002) · Repository: github.com/notomodo/adtc-2026

This session measured the generation half of the system for the first time — whether the local LLM actually uses the chunks retrieval hands it. Three grounding prompts were tested against the same 41 questions. The result: one large regression, one diagnosed root cause, and a final prompt that answers correctly on 96% of the questions where retrieval succeeds, while never once fabricating a business policy.

  • The grounding contract holds. When given the correct chunk, the model uses it correctly 27 times out of 28. When not given it, the model abstains rather than inventing. Both halves of the intended behaviour are now measured rather than assumed.
  • Zero fabrication events. Across three prompt versions and 18 probe evaluations, the model never once invented a policy for the business when the corpus was silent. This was the single most important safety property to establish, and it held under three materially different prompts.
  • A large regression was caused, diagnosed, and reversed. The v2 prompt halved performance (45.7% → 17.1%). The root cause — prompt salience acting as a behavioural prior on a small model — is a transferable engineering finding, and the failed run is retained in the record.
  • The remaining shortfall is retrieval, not generation. Four of the five residual abstentions are correct: the answer was never placed in the context window. All four are prose/legal-inference questions — the same weakness independently measured in the retrieval study.

2. What was measured, and how

The generation layer had been entirely unquantified. Retrieval quality (does the system find the right passage) says nothing about whether the LLM then uses it — a chunk can rank first and the model can still answer wrongly, or ignore it, or hallucinate over it.

Benchmark

35 answerable questions from the v3 benchmark set, each with a labelled gold chunk, stratified into five kinds: exact_fact, paraphrase, near_miss, prose (legal inference), and multi_chunk (requires synthesis across passages).

Plus 6 purpose-built "unanswerable" probes (U01–U06) — plausible SME questions the corpus genuinely does not answer: international shipping, instalment payments, warranty duration, physical stores, loyalty programmes, delivery times. Absence was verified by keyword scan against the corpus. These exist because the 35 answerable questions cannot test abstention at all — every one has an answer, so the model is never given a chance to correctly refuse.

Why the probes matter most For a business document assistant, the dangerous failure is not "I don't know" — it is a fluent, confident, wrong answer about the business's own policy. If the corpus says returns close after 2 days and the model supplies a generic "usually 14–30 days," that misinforms a business owner about their own terms. The probes are the only part of the benchmark that tests this directly.

Grading

Two layers were built. Layer A is deterministic and model-free: token-overlap against the gold chunk plus abstention detection. Layer B is an LLM judge. Layer B was measured in the first run and found unreliable — 46% agreement with Layer A, because Qwen judging Qwen shares its own blind spots and it frequently echoed the abstention token instead of using its verdict labels. It was excluded from subsequent runs. All figures in this report come from Layer A applied identically, in a single execution, to all three answer sets.

Honest limitation on every number here Layer A is a heuristic, not a truth oracle. "Pass" means the answer materially overlaps the gold chunk — not that it is factually correct and complete. Per the project's standing rule that substring grounding is a pre-filter only, a hand-read sample of the passing answers is required before 71.4% is quoted as an accuracy figure. That review is outstanding.
Update — 2026-07-23: that review is done. Of a 13-item adversarial sample, 9/10 sampled PASSes were confirmed correct and 1 confirmed ungrounded (Q19). See R5_validation_result.md. 71.4% remains Layer A's automated pass rate, not a validated accuracy figure.

3. The three prompts and what happened

v1 — numbered rules, with a general-knowledge note

Strict grounding rules 1–5, an abstention token NOT_IN_DOCUMENTS, and permission to add a clearly-labelled [GENERAL KNOWLEDGE — not from the documents] note after abstaining. Result: 16/35 (45.7%). The characteristic bug was self-contradiction — on 9 questions the model emitted the abstention token and then answered correctly anyway, with the gold chunk retrieved at rank 1.

v2 — restructured, more explicit — and a large regression

A rewrite into a two-step procedure (STEP 1 — DECIDE / STEP 2 — ACT) with two output forms, keeping the general-knowledge note. Intended to remove the ambiguity that caused v1's self-contradiction. Result: 6/35 (17.1%) — performance more than halved, with 27 of 35 answerable questions abstained.

The new failure mode was stranger and more insidious than v1's. Q10 illustrates it — the same question under both prompts:

v1: Innerwear, swimsuits, gym wear, sleep wear; Perfumes, jewelry, health & wellness products, adult toys ← correct, grounded v2: NOT_IN_DOCUMENTS [GENERAL KNOWLEDGE — not from the documents]: Innerwear, swimsuits, gym wear, sleep wear; Perfumes, jewelry, health & wellness products, adult toys ← same answer, mislabelled

The model retrieved the right chunk, read it, extracted the right answer — then declared it was not in the documents and relabelled its own document-grounded answer as outside knowledge. 20 of the 27 v2 abstentions carried the real answer inside the note, with 100% correct label formatting. It had learned the format perfectly and used it to launder correct answers.

Root cause — a transferable finding In v2 the abstain branch was longer, more detailed and more emphatic than the answer branch. For a 3-billion-parameter model, prompt salience acts as a behavioural prior, not merely as a constraint. Elaborating the rule for the behaviour you do not want makes that behaviour more likely; length and emphasis are themselves signals. The attempt to constrain abstention more precisely made abstention more attractive.

v3 — invert the salience, remove the note

The v2 failure produced a specific, falsifiable hypothesis: the general-knowledge note is itself the attractor — it gave the model a sanctioned free-text slot that competed with answering. v3 removed the slot entirely and inverted the balance: a long, prominent instruction to answer directly (explicitly licensing synthesis across passages, partial answers, and wording mismatches between question and passage), with abstention compressed to one line and a bare token.

The refutation condition was pre-registered before the run: if pass ≤ 16/35, the hypothesis is refuted and further prompt-tuning on this model is low-yield. Observed: 25/35.

5. The corrected denominator — what the headline hides

The 25/35 figure conflates two systems. Retrieval delivers three chunks; generation works with what it is given. If retrieval never surfaces the gold chunk, generation cannot be charged for the miss.

Checking the retrieved chunk IDs against the gold labels for every question: retrieval placed the gold chunk into the k=3 context on only 28 of the 35 answerable questions. On the other seven, the model was never shown the answer.

Figure 3 — Attributing the shortfall. Of the 28 questions where retrieval succeeded, generation behaved correctly on 27. The remaining seven are retrieval failures.

The number that characterises generation Conditioned on what retrieval provides, generation is correct on 27 of 28 questions — 96%. Four of the five residual abstentions (Q17, Q27, Q29, Q35) are correct behaviour: the gold chunk was absent from the context window and the model said so. Counting them as generation failures understates the system. All four are prose/legal-inference questions — the same weakness measured independently in the retrieval study (prose Recall@5 = 62%).

The single genuine generation defect is Q08 ("Can a teenager open an account?"), where the gold chunk was retrieved at rank 1 and the model abstained regardless. One failure in 28. It is documented rather than fixed — chasing it would cost another 45-minute run and risk a fresh regression.

6. Safety: the abstention property

All six unanswerable probes abstained correctly under every prompt version — v1, v2, and v3 alike. Removing the general-knowledge slot in v3 carried a real risk of over-correction: an instruction to "answer directly" could have bled into fabricating facts absent from context. It did not.

Figure 4 — The abstention safety property held under three materially different prompts. 18 probe evaluations, zero fabrication events.

This is the strongest result in the session. A property that survives three substantially different prompts is considerably better evidenced than one that passes a single run — the prompts varied enormously in structure, length and emphasis, and the behaviour was invariant across all of them.

7. Method integrity

  • Committed retriever reused, not reimplemented — the harness imports the locked HybridRetriever directly; retrieval logic was untouched across all three runs.
  • Fully offline — local embedder from cache, local Ollama; no network, no API keys.
  • Deterministic decode — temperature=0, seed=42. Each run was a single continuous warm process (generation is not bit-identical across an Ollama model reload).
  • Fingerprint gate — corpus fingerprint 592a602f845dce20 verified on every run.
  • One variable changed at a time — only the SYSTEM_PROMPT string differed between runs; the question set, retriever, k, model and decode settings were held constant.
  • Falsification pre-registered — the v3 refutation condition (pass ≤ 16/35) was fixed before the run, not after seeing the result.
  • Common grader — all three answer sets were graded by the same Layer A code in one execution, so the numbers are directly comparable.

8. Findings, stated plainly

  1. The general-knowledge note was the attractor. Removing it eliminated 19 laundered answers and raised the pass rate by 26 points. The hypothesis was pre-registered and supported.
  2. Prompt salience is a behavioural prior on small models. Making the abstain branch longer and more emphatic made abstention more likely, not more precise. This generalises well beyond this project.
  3. Generation is not the bottleneck — retrieval is. At 96% conditioned on retrieval, generation is performing near its ceiling. The prose/legal-inference retrieval gap is the binding constraint.
  4. The safety property is robust. Zero fabrications across 18 probe evaluations spanning three prompts.
  5. An LLM judging its own outputs is unreliable here. 46% agreement with a deterministic checker. The deterministic anchor caught the judge failing — which is precisely why the blended design was chosen over an LLM judge alone.

9. Decision and open items

DECISION-004: lock the v3 grounding prompt. It strictly dominates v1 and v2 on every measured axis, with no metric favouring either predecessor.

Accepted trade-off v3 has no general-knowledge note. The product now either answers from the documents or abstains with a bare token — losing the "abstain, then optionally add a labelled aside" behaviour originally specified. Given direct evidence that the note pulls a small model into mislabelling correct answers, a bare unambiguous abstention is safer and easier to defend. If the note is ever reintroduced, it must be re-tested against v3 as the baseline.

Open items

  • Hand-read ~8–10 of the 25 passing answers, weighted toward prose and multi_chunk. Blocking before 71.4% is quoted as an accuracy figure anywhere. Done 2026-07-23 — 9/10 sampled PASSes confirmed correct, 1 confirmed ungrounded (Q19). See R5_validation_result.md.
  • Selective reranking (DECISION-003) — the outstanding lever for the prose retrieval gap; fires the cross-encoder only on low-confidence queries so most queries pay no latency.
  • Gold labels on the 16 newer questions and all 6 probes remain single-annotator and unverified against the source PDFs.
  • Q08 — one unexplained generation failure with the gold chunk present; documented, not fixed.
  • No measurements on the deployment reference machine — all latency figures (~65–105 s/question) come from the pessimistic i5-4300U floor.
